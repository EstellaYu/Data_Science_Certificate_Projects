What lives in your belly botton?

-- You will be surprised!

Back in 2010, a curious undergrad in North Carolina State University had a random idea -- sampling colleague's navel bacteria for a holiday card. What's better than ones' belly button -- as one of the body's most rarely scrubbed crannies, it may offer researchers a chance to study as close to a pristine microbial landscape as is possible on the modern human.

The result is just astonishing...

  • In the original sample collected from 60 individuals, 2,368 bacteria species are found, 1,458 of which may be new to science.
  • Some belly buttons harbored as few as 29 species and some as many as 107, although most had around 67.
  • 92% of the bacteria types showed up on fewer than 10% of subjects — in fact, most of the time, they appeared in only a single subject.
  • One science writer, for instance, apparently harbored a bacterium that had previously been found only in soil from Japan—where he has never been.
(Welcome to the Jungle!)
Although the data collection for Belly Botton Biodiversity Project has stopped in 2012, data collected from 135 individuals are available online, and are readily available HERE.

In addition to inspiring scientific curiosity, Belly Button Biodiversity inspired conversations about the beneficial roles microbes play in our daily lives.
Maybe it will enhance the way you appreciate nature and the eco-balance in your body, too. Enjoy!
